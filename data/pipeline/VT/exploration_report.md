## Program Details

### Vermont Senior Companion Program

> **NEW** — not currently in our data

**Eligibility:**
- Age: 55+
- Income: Based on 200% of federal poverty guidelines. Examples: 2022 levels - $27,180 (1 person), $36,620 (2), $46,060 (3), $55,500 (4); other sources cite approx. $30,120 (single), $40,880 (couple), $51,640 (3), $62,400 (4), or $31,300 (single), $42,300 (couple). Levels updated annually; contact agency for current table.[1][2][4]
- Assets: No asset limits mentioned in available information.
- Able to serve at least 10 hours per week (up to 40 hours)
- Available through Vermont’s Area Agencies on Aging

**Benefits:** Non-financial assistance and friendship to elderly adults (age 60+) facing difficulty with daily tasks like shopping, paying bills, errands, rides; promotes independence and provides caregiver respite. For companions: $4.00/hour non-taxable stipend (does not affect benefits like 3SquaresVT or housing), $0.70/mile reimbursement, flexible 10-40 hours/week, training (orientation + 4 hours/month in-service), monthly gatherings, support.[1][2]
- Varies by: fixed

**How to apply:**
- Contact Area Agencies on Aging: Age Well (www.agewellvt.org), Northeast Kingdom Council on Aging (www.nekcouncil.org), Senior Solutions (www.seniorsolutionsvt.org), Southwestern Vermont Council on Aging (www.svcoa.org)
- Helpline: 1-800-642-5119[2]

**Timeline:** Not specified in sources.

**Watch out for:**
- This is a volunteer program for low-income seniors (55+) to help other elderly; families do not 'qualify' or 'apply' directly for services—their loved one must be matched with a companion volunteer. Income eligibility uses approx. 200% poverty level (updates yearly). Stipend non-taxable and benefit-protected. Not a paid job or direct client service signup.[1][2]
- Income examples from sources vary slightly by year; verify current with agency.

**Data shape:** Volunteer companion program (not direct client benefits); eligibility for companions only; income at ~200% FPL with annual updates; statewide via 4 regional agencies; no asset test or client application process.

**Source:** No single primary .gov URL; program via AmeriCorps Seniors, administered by Vermont Area Agencies on Aging (e.g., www.agewellvt.org).[1][2]
